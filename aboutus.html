About Adidas

Adidas is a global sportswear brand that was founded in Germany by Adolf “Adi” Dassler. In the early 1920s, Adi began making athletic shoes in his mother’s laundry room with the goal of helping athletes improve their performance. In 1924, he and his brother Rudolf established the Dassler Brothers Shoe Factory, and their shoes gained international recognition when athletes, including Jesse Owens at the 1936 Berlin Olympics, wore them during major competitions. After the brothers separated due to personal disagreements, Adi founded Adidas in 1949 and introduced the now-famous three-stripe logo. The company grew rapidly by creating innovative sports footwear, such as football boots with removable studs, and later expanded into apparel and accessories. Over the decades, Adidas became not only a leading performance brand in sports but also a major influence in fashion and street culture. Today, Adidas continues to focus on innovation, sustainability, and empowering athletes around the world.

Our Mission

Adidas mission is to be the best sports brand in the world by creating innovative products that help athletes perform at their highest level. The company focuses on performance, quality, design, and sustainability, aiming to meet the needs of athletes and consumers through continuous innovation and creativity.

Our Vision

Adidas vision is centered around the belief that “through sport, we have the power to change lives. ” The brand strives to inspire and empower people worldwide by promoting sport, inclusivity, and positive impact in communities while driving sustainability and responsible business practices.
